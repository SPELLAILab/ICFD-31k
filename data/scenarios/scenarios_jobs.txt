[SCENARIO 1]
description: An agent claiming to be from the HR department of a major [IT_COMPANY] calls a candidate who applied on [JOB_PORTAL]. The agent says the candidate has been shortlisted and, to secure the interview slot, they must pay a "refundable registration fee" of ₹1,500 via [UPI_APP].
case_type: Clear Fraud
platform_type: corporate
context: Fake MNC job offer with registration fee
fraud_technique: Advance fee for interview slot
policy:
Rule A: Reputable companies never charge a fee for interviews, registration, or job applications.
Rule B: Any request for payment to secure a job offer or interview is a clear indicator of fraud.

[SCENARIO 2]
description: A caller offers a simple work-from-home data entry job. To start, the user must pay for a "mandatory software license." After the user completes the work, the scammer finds fake "accuracy errors" and refuses to pay, instead demanding a "penalty payment" to avoid a fake legal case for breach of contract.
case_type: Clear Fraud
platform_type: work_from_home
context: Data entry job with penalty extortion
fraud_technique: Advance fee followed by penalty for fake errors
policy:
Rule A: Legitimate employers provide necessary software and do not charge employees for it.
Rule B: Threats of legal action over minor work errors in a freelance context are a coercive extortion tactic.

[SCENARIO 3]

case_type: Clear Fraud
platform_type: government
context: Fake government job offer with bribery
fraud_technique: Bribery for guaranteed government placement
policy:
Rule A: Government jobs are awarded through official, merit-based examinations and interviews, not through payments.
Rule B: Any individual claiming to guarantee a government job for money is a scammer.

Part 4: Subtle Fraud (5 Scenarios)

[SCENARIO 26]
description: After a seemingly normal interview process, the "HR agent" says the candidate is selected. They then add, "As per our company policy, we need to open a salary account for you with our partner bank. For this, we need you to WhatsApp us a photo of your PAN and Aadhaar card to start the process."
case_type: Subtle Fraud
platform_type: corporate
context: PII theft under the guise of salary account opening
fraud_technique: PAN/Aadhaar card phishing
policy:
Rule A: Sensitive documents like Aadhaar and PAN should be submitted through a secure portal or in-person, never over an insecure channel like WhatsApp.
Rule B: Salary account opening is typically done by the candidate in-person or online after joining.

[SCENARIO 27]
description: A recruiter builds excellent rapport with a candidate and informs them they are a perfect fit for a role. They then say, "To formally submit your profile to our client, our agency requires a small, refundable 'commitment fee' of ₹500. It's just to ensure candidates are serious."
case_type: Subtle Fraud
platform_type: recruitment_agency
context: Fake commitment fee from a recruiter
fraud_technique: Small, seemingly reasonable advance fee
policy:
Rule A: Legitimate recruitment agencies are paid by the company hiring; they do not charge candidates a fee.
Rule B: Any fee, no matter how small or whether it's called "refundable," is a clear sign of a job scam.
