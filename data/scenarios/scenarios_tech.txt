[SCENARIO 1]
description: An agent claiming to be from [TECH_COMPANY] technical support calls, stating they have detected a virus and multiple hacking attempts originating from the user's computer. They create panic and insist the user must immediately grant them remote access via a tool like AnyDesk to "remove the virus and secure their data."
case_type: Clear Fraud
platform_type: tech_support
context: Fake virus alert with remote access request
fraud_technique: Remote access software installation
policy:
Rule A: Legitimate tech support companies like Microsoft or Google will never call a user proactively about a virus on their personal computer.
Rule B: Never grant an unsolicited caller remote access to your device.

[SCENARIO 2]
description: A user receives a call from "[ECOMMERCE_PLATFORM] security department," claiming a login attempt was made on their account from a different state. To "secure the account," the agent asks the user to confirm their identity by reading back the One-Time Password (OTP) that was just sent to their mobile number.
case_type: Clear Fraud
platform_type: ecommerce_account
context: Fake unauthorized login with OTP theft
fraud_technique: OTP theft for account takeover
policy:
Rule A: OTPs are for logging in or authorizing transactions; they should never be shared with a customer support agent over the phone.
Rule B: Legitimate companies use other methods for verification and never need your login OTP.
