[SCENARIO 1]
description: An agent from a fake travel company ([TRAVEL_COMPANY]) calls, congratulating the user for winning a "free" 3-night holiday package to Goa. To claim the prize, the user is told they must pay a mandatory "GST and processing charge" of ₹5,000 immediately via [UPI_APP].
case_type: Clear Fraud
platform_type: travel_agency
context: Fake holiday package with advance fee
fraud_technique: Advance fee for a non-existent prize
policy:
Rule A: Legitimate prizes or winnings do not require the winner to pay an advance fee, tax, or processing charge.
Rule B: Unsolicited calls announcing a "free" prize are a major red flag for a scam.

[SCENARIO 2]
description: A scammer calls, claiming to be from the famous TV show [LOTTERY_NAME] (e.g., Kaun Banega Crorepati). They announce that the user's mobile number was selected in a lucky draw and they have won ₹25 lakhs. To transfer the prize money, they ask for the user's bank account details and instruct them to first pay a "tax deduction fee" to a personal account.

Rule A: Official lotteries deduct taxes from the prize money before awarding it; they do not ask the winner to pay the tax upfront.
Rule B: Never share full bank account details with an unsolicited caller.
