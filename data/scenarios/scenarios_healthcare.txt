[SCENARIO 1]
description: An agent claiming to be from [INSURANCE_COMPANY] calls, stating the user's life insurance policy has lapsed due to a missed premium. They create urgency, saying all benefits will be lost. To "reactivate it instantly" and avoid penalties, the user must pay the premium amount plus a "reactivation fee" to the agent's personal [UPI_APP] ID.
case_type: Clear Fraud
platform_type: insurance_provider
context: Fake policy lapse with immediate payment demand
fraud_technique: Advance fee for fake policy reactivation
policy:
Rule A: Premium payments must only be made through official channels (company website, app, official payment links), never to an agent's personal account.
Rule B: Legitimate companies provide a grace period for missed premiums and send official written communication.

[SCENARIO 2]
description: A caller impersonating an official from the "IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority) Grievance Department" claims a large bonus or maturity fund is pending on the user's old policy. To release this fund, the user must first pay a "processing fee" or "GST" to a specific bank account.
case_type: Clear Fraud
platform_type: government_impersonation
context: Fake bonus payout with advance fee
fraud_technique: Advance fee for releasing a non-existent bonus
policy:
Rule A: Insurance bonuses or maturity amounts are paid out automatically and never require an upfront fee, tax, or charge to be paid by the customer.
Rule B: IRDAI is a regulatory body and does not get involved in individual bonus payouts.

[SCENARIO 3]
description: A scammer calls offering a "government-approved" [GOVERNMENT_SCHEME] health card for a very low one-time fee of ₹2,500. They promise it will provide lifetime coverage for all diseases at any hospital in India. They ask for the payment to be made immediately to get the card "activated." The policy is non-existent.
case_type: Clear Fraud
platform_type: government_scheme
context: Fake government health card offer
fraud_technique: Selling a non-existent insurance product
policy:
Rule A: Official government health schemes (like Ayushman Bharat) have specific eligibility criteria and enrollment is done through official centers, not via unsolicited phone calls.
Rule B: Be wary of offers that sound too good to be true, such as lifetime coverage for a small one-time fee.
